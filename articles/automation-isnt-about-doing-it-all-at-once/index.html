Automation Isn't About Doing It All at Once

automation process-improvement strategy

Most businesses think automation means a big, expensive project that takes months to finish. When you try to automate everything at once, you usually end up with a tangled mess that nobody knows how to fix. There is a faster way to do this: the Minimum Effective Automation (MEA.)

An MEA is the exact opposite of a bloated tech project. It is based entirely on the 80/20 rule: we apply the minimum amount of automation required to give you the maximum amount of value. Instead of trying to automate your entire business, we find the one critical gap and plug it.

When author Tim Ferriss learns a new skill, he uses a framework called DiSSS (Deconstruction, Selection, Sequencing, and Stakes). The core idea is to deconstruct a complex subject into its raw parts, then ruthlessly select and simplify the 20% of the work that delivers 80% of the results. We use this exact same logic for business automation, so you only focus on the vital few steps that are costing you the most time and money.

Our MEA Methodology

We have adapted this approach into a five-step process to get your tools talking to each other quickly and reliably.

1. Find the Pain

Automation without a clear problem is just busywork. We start by pinpointing the exact spot where your process breaks, or where leads are going cold because follow-up fell through the cracks.

2. Deconstruct the Process

We break your current manual process down into its individual steps. We look at every form submission, every email you send, and every manual copy-pasting task to see exactly what is happening.

3. Simplify

This is the critical 80/20 step. Most agencies get it wrong by trying to automate a messy process exactly as it is. We strip your process back to the absolute essentials, cutting any action that doesn't directly drive the outcome. ​

4. Build the Bot

We wire up your core apps using n8n. If you use tools like Pipedrive, Xero, and Slack, we connect them so the moment a trigger happens, the action fires instantly.

5. Prove and Improve

We turn the system on and see how it behaves with real customers. When the core MEA is steady, we can add the next piece.
